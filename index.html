what and why?

The web is a translucent atmosphere for our social, political, and cultural lives. Documentary evidence of our experience is fruitful territory for understanding the world in more nuanced, inviting, and inclusive ways.

However, the web itself is inherently transitory and non-persistent. To explore it in the future, you can gather it for yourself now. Harvesting web content is a method of building collections.

Next

who?

Web content can be gathered, preserved, and shared at widely differing scales. These processes have elements in common: select, capture, store, and use.

When you harvest web content, you do so on behalf of your present self, your future self, and all those with whom you share your work. Documenting context for collections you harvest generates information about the content: this is metadata.
